Welcome to True Care System

True Care System is an enterprise care management platform designed for organizations that provide home care, intellectual and developmental disability services, residential support, community-based services, and other person-centered programs.

The platform brings workforce management, individual records, scheduling, medication, behavioral support, authorizations, reporting, KPI management, time keeping, EVV, visit maintenance, house management, payroll, billing, HIPAA, support, and provider administration into one coordinated system.

Purpose

The Getting Started guide introduces the system and explains the information users need before beginning daily work.

After completing this section, users should be able to:

  • Access True Care System securely.
  • Understand the main platform structure.
  • Recognize the purpose of each major module.
  • Understand that access depends on assigned roles and permissions.
  • Follow basic security and HIPAA responsibilities.
  • Continue to the documentation relevant to their job duties.

True Care System Login Page

The login page is the secure entry point for authorized users. Users must enter their individually assigned credentials before accessing protected system functions or information.

Who Should Use This Documentation

  • Provider Administrators manage users, roles, departments, branches, provider settings, and organization-level configuration.
  • Office Staff maintain operational records, schedules, employees, individuals, visits, and supporting data.
  • Supervisors review service delivery, documentation, scheduling, employee activity, and exceptions.
  • DSPs and Nurses use assigned operational and care workflows based on their permissions.
  • Billing and Payroll Staff review readiness, visits, rates, submissions, work time, and payment processing.
  • Compliance and Security Staff review HIPAA, access, audit logs, incidents, and security events.
  • Developers and QA Staff use technical, API, testing, and release documentation.

Users may see different menus, pages, fields, and actions depending on their role, provider assignment, permissions, and system configuration.

How Access Is Controlled

True Care System uses role-based access control. A user's visible menus and available actions are determined by assigned roles, permissions, provider membership, and account status.

Typical roles include:

  • Provider Administrator
  • Supervisor
  • Office
  • DSP
  • Nurse
  • Behavioral
  • Billing
  • Payroll
  • HR
  • Compliance
  • Facility
  • IT
  • Read Only
Important

The presence of a menu does not automatically grant permission to every page or action within that menu.

Before You Begin

Confirm the following before accessing production information:

  • Your organization has an active True Care System account.
  • Your user account was created by an authorized administrator.
  • Your assigned role matches your job responsibilities.
  • You received the correct login address.
  • You are using a supported browser and secure internet connection.
  • You understand your organization's privacy and security policies.
  • You know how to contact an administrator or Support.

Security and HIPAA Responsibilities

True Care System may contain Protected Health Information and other confidential organizational information. Every user is responsible for protecting information available through the account.

  • Use only the account assigned to you.
  • Keep passwords and verification codes confidential.
  • Access only information needed for assigned work.
  • Lock or sign out of unattended devices.
  • Avoid displaying PHI where unauthorized persons can view it.
  • Report suspected unauthorized access or disclosure promptly.
Minimum Necessary

Access only the minimum information required to complete the assigned task.

Audit Logging

True Care System uses audit logging to support accountability, compliance review, security monitoring, and investigation.

Depending on the feature and system configuration, records may include:

  • Successful and failed login attempts.
  • Logout and session expiration.
  • Password and account security activity.
  • Access to protected records.
  • Creation, update, deletion, and status changes.
  • Support impersonation activity.
  • Billing, payroll, and administrative actions.
  • Exports and other high-risk operations.

Best Practices

  • Use a supported, current browser.
  • Do not share accounts or credentials.
  • Review system messages before continuing.
  • Verify information before selecting Save, Submit, Delete, or Approve.
  • Keep employee and individual information accurate.
  • Use organization-approved devices and networks.
  • Sign out when work is complete.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do all users see the same menus?

No. Visible menus and available actions depend on the user's role, permissions, provider assignment, and system configuration.

Can multiple employees share one account?

No. Each user must have an individually assigned account so access and activity can be secured and audited.

What should I do if a required menu is missing?

Contact an authorized Provider Administrator. The account may not have the required role or permission.

Should screenshots contain real individual information?

No. Documentation screenshots must use demo or training data and must not expose real PHI, credentials, Social Security numbers, full Medicaid IDs, or private notes.
